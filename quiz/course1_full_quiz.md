## Q46. [MEDIUM] Ch5 Intraday Patterns — Trap Patterns
What is a liquidity trap?
- A) A stock that has zero volume
- B) A stock trading only on the NYSE
- C) Price sweeps above/below a key level, triggering retail stops, then immediately reverses
- D) A stock with a bid-ask spread of exactly $0.01

*Answer: C*
**Explanation:** Liquidity trap: price sweeps above/below a key level (often stop-hunting), triggering retail traders' stops, then immediately reverses. The market maker or institution has taken the opposite side of all those stopped-out trades.
*Source: c5_intraday_patterns_rules.md P5*

A candle on your 1-minute chart is still forming — it has 45 seconds left. What should you do?
- A) Act immediately based on the current candle shape
- B) Wait — don't act on incomplete candles; wait for the candle to close and confirm
- C) Enter on the close of the previous candle

## Q48. [EASY] Ch5 Intraday Patterns — Pivot Rules
What are pivot points and how are they labeled?
- A) Moving averages: 9 EMA, 20 EMA, 50 EMA
- B) Calculated S/R levels: R3 > R2 > R1 > Pivot > S1 > S2 > S3
- C) Candlestick patterns: hammer, doji, engulfing
- D) Volume levels: high volume nodes

*Answer: B*
**Explanation:** Pivots = calculated S/R levels based on prior day's H/L/close. Labels: R3 > R2 > R1 > Pivot > S1 > S2 > S3. Price bouncing between levels = range-bound. Breaking through = trend day.

## Q49. [EASY] Risk Management — Position Sizing
What is the 2:1 risk/reward rule and why does it matter?
- A) You need $2 profit for every $1 risk — without this minimum, the trade doesn't have enough edge
- B) You risk $2 for every $1 in your account
- C) You take 2 trades for every 1 you skip
- D) The stop must be 2× the target

*Answer: A*
**Explanation:** 2:1 minimum: profit target must be 2× the stop distance. $0.20 target requires ≤$0.10 stop. If stop = $0.30, you need $0.60 target just to hit 2:1. Without 2:1, the math of trading works against you.
*Source: c15_trading_plan_rules.md §3*

- A) Any news from the past month
- B) News from the past year about the company
- C) News within the last 1-2 hours — active, right-now catalyst
- D) Social media posts about the stock

**Explanation:** P2 Catalyst: Fresh breaking news within the last 1-2 hours (ideally <60 min old). Old news = faded catalyst. In hot markets: no-news but obvious stock = acceptable as 2nd priority.
