4. Creating Tax Free Income for the Future 
 In the US you can trade in a regular account where you take out the proﬁts right 
away. In this account, you’ll pay income tax. 
 However, you can also trade in an IRA (individual retirement account). In these 
accounts, there is no income tax on the proﬁt you make. 
 For the reason, every trader should have a regular account for income to cover cost 
of living, and an IRA account for growing tax free proﬁts. 
Trading in a Tax Free Account & Reinvesting 
Day Trading Proﬁts 
 In my opinion, you’d be doing yourself a disservice if you don’t reinvest some of your 
day trading proﬁts in order to capitalize on long term growth. 
 
I actively day trade in my retirement account to grow it quickly and reinvest the proﬁt 
for long term growth.  
Compound Interest at 8-10% per year 
If I can earn $200/day, I’ll be saving and reinvesting $50k of my proﬁts/year TAX 
FREE.  If I do that for 10 years I’ll have saved $500,000 but it will grow to over 
$869,193 thanks to dividends and compound interest. 
If I’m able to do it for 20 years, $1mil saved = $2.7mil .  
The growth curve increases exponentially as you begin earning compound interest 
on your earlier gains.  The earlier you start, the more you’ll have! 

When I Reinvest Day Trading Proﬁts, I move them to 
LOWER RISK long term investments. 
 Lowest Risk/Return  = Passive Long Term for Dividends Investing returning 5-10% return/year 
Highest Risk/Return  = Day Trading can return 100%+ per year or even per month with small 
accounts 
 From $583 to $100k in 45 days was over 17,000%  return. 
 From $583 to $1mil was over 170,000% return. 
 From $583 to $10 million was 1,700,000%  return. 
These returns would not be scalable to $50k, $500k, or $5mil account starting balances. 
  

Moving Proﬁts from High Risk to Low Risk 
 Most traders keep anywhere from $5k-100k in their active trading accounts. 
 By consistently reinvesting proﬁts (instead of just spending them) after 5-10 years of 
proﬁtable trading and active reinvesting, you can actually earn more money on 
dividends from passive income than from active trading . 
 This is the ultimate in ﬁnancial independence. 
  
Alternative Investments in a Self Directed IRA 
 You can invest in Real-Estate and other asset classes with self-directed IRAs. 
 This allows you to diversify your investment portfolio and not be 100% concentrated 
in equities. 
   
4. How much money do YOU  need to live comfortably? 
How much money would you need saved in the bank to retire today? 
Everyone’s answer is unique to their personal circumstances, but there are 
popular formulas used to ﬁnd this magic number! 

The Rule of 4% 
The rule of 4% states that you can withdraw 4%/yr from an account 
invested in the stock market forever, without depleting the account. 
How is that possible? 
The average account growth is 7-10% per year, historically, so when you 
draw 4% the account is still able to continue to keep up with inﬂation and 
not lose value. 

The Rule of 300 and the Rule of 4% 
This rule states that your monthly expenses x 300 is the amount of 
money needed to retire. 
What is your magic retirement number? 
If I spend $5k/mo ($60k/yr) I need to have… 
$5k x 300 = $1.5mil in savings to retire comfortably.  
$1.5mil x 4%/yr = $60,000.

6. Why do Traders Fail? 
Leading causes of failure 
1. Lack of Strategy 
2. Inability to maintain the discipline required to follow the RULES of the strategy. 
Secondary contributors to failure 
1. Disposition not aligned with the risk taking nature of trading 
2. Aptitude for making quick decisions and using computer software 
3. Using incorrect equipment (trading on mobile app) 
4. Inability to concentrate, absorb and retain new information 
5. Lack of emotional intelligence (inability to recognize emotions of fear, greed, anger, frustration, in self or others)

7. Setting Your Deﬁnition of Success 
What does success in trading mean to you? 
How much money do you want to be making per day, per month, per year? 
Are you prepared for the reality that in the ﬁrst 3-6 months of following the trading plan I’ve 
prepared for you, you will NOT be earning any signiﬁcant proﬁt. (The ﬁrst 3-6 months in SIM 
trading then proof of concept trading with small size in a real-money account). 
Can you have the discipline to follow the path laid out for you? 

The ﬁrst year is about survival 
As a beginner trader most people set a goal of $100-200/day trading. But this is not about 
making a lot of money. It’s about proof of concept. Can you be consistent? 
Survive till you thrive. The longer you can keep your head above water (avoiding blow ups, 
not taking unnecessary risks, preventing big losses), the more time you’ll be able to spend in 
the market. At a certain point, learning to survive will teach you how to thrive. You’ll learn 
what NOT to do. 

Dedicate yourself to the Path to Success 
1.Don’t trade with real money until you have proven that you can make money for at 
least 1 month in a simulated trading environment. 
2. Ask yourself: How am I going to approach this new endeavour?  There will be times 
you are frustrated, exhausted, and confused.  Be forgiving towards yourself.  You are 
working to learn something that is NOT easy.
