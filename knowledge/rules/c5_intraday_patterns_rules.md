## P1: INTRAJAY TIMEFRAMES
- Primary: **1-minute** and **5-minute** charts
- Secondary: 10-second (for precise entry timing), 15-minute (context)
- All patterns exist on all timeframes; the *time of day* and *where in the session* matters
- Real-time candle formation is fluid — the candle signal only becomes fixed at close
- "45 seconds left in the candle" = keep watching; don't act on incomplete candles

## P2: BULL FLAG RULES
- **Formation**: Initial spike up (1+ green candles, ideally increasing volume) followed by 2+ red candles pulling back
  - Red candles CANNOT break the high of the previous candle
- **Volume profile**: HIGH volume on spike candles, LIGHT volume on pullback candles, volume INCREASES as price makes a new high on the continuation
  - If inverted (light spike, heavy pullback) = distribution, DO NOT enter
- **Entry**: First candle making a NEW HIGH after the pullback
  - Alternative: enter at the moment of flat-top breakout if you caught the consolidation earlier
- **Stop**: Below the pullback low (aggressive) or below the flag pole base (conservative)
- **Target**: First resistance zone above; Ross often exits on first sign of reversal
- **Context**: Must have a catalyst driving the initial spike (scanner-triggering move)

## P3: BEAR FLAG RULES (inverse of Bull Flag)
- **Formation**: Initial drop (1+ red candles on heavy volume) followed by 2+ green candles pulling back up
  - Green candles CANNOT break the low of the previous candle
- **Volume profile**: HIGH volume on drop, LIGHT volume on bounce, volume INCREASES on continuation down
- **Entry**: First candle making a NEW LOW after the pullback
- **Short sellers**: Trade stocks below VWAP, short on pops, look for fade back to VWAP

## P4: VWAP BREAKOUT RULES
- **Above VWAP** = bulls in control → bias long
- **Below VWAP** = bears in control → bias short
- **VWAP breakout**: Price crosses from below to above = shift in control from sellers to buyers
- **Trigger**: Shorts forced to cover + long traders jump in → first pullback back to VWAP = first entry
- **High relative volume** → VWAP becomes critical battleground (more volume = more important)
- **False breakout**: Stock can squeeze so fast that VWAP sits right under current price → pullback can happen violently
- **Extended move warning**: "Very extended off the 9 MA = increases risk of false breakout" → tighter stop needed

## P5: TRAP PATTERNS (Bull Trap / Bear Trap / Liquidity Trap / Jackknife Candle)
- **Bull Trap**: Price breaks above resistance on low volume, then immediately reverses down
  - High volume on the reversal = confirmed trap, exit immediately
- **Bear Trap**: Price breaks below support on low volume, then reverses up
- **Liquidity Trap**: Price sweeps above/below key level (stop hunt), triggering retail stops, then reverses
  - "The second it failed there, if it started to drop, I would have gotten out"
- **Jackknife Candle**: Sharp, sudden reversal candle — large body, small wicks — extreme momentum reversal signal
- **Rule**: If caught in a trap → exit immediately, small winner or small loser = both are correct outcomes
  - Do NOT average down, do NOT hold hoping for recovery

## P6: ABCD PATTERN RULES
- Price moves from A to B, pulls back to C, then moves to D (same distance as A→B)
- C typically retraces 38.2%–78.6% of AB
- Entry at C confirmation → target = D (equal to AB distance)
- Works on all timeframes; 5-min is most practical for day trading

## P7: HOD/LOD BREAKOUT RULES
- **HOD Breakout**: Price breaks above today's High of Day on increasing volume → continuation long
- **LOD Breakdown**: Price breaks below today's Low of Day on increasing volume → continuation short
- **Flat Top Breakout**: Price consolidates at high, breaks out sideways → same as HOD but horizontal
- **Flat Bottom Breakdown**: Price consolidates at low, breaks down → same as LOD but horizontal
- **Entry**: First candle making new HOD/LOD after consolidation
- **Context**: HOD/LOD breakouts often happen later in the day; be patient

## P8: PIVOT RULES
- Pivots = calculated support/resistance levels based on prior day's H/L/close
- Daily pivots: R3 > R2 > R1 > Pivot > S1 > S2 > S3
- Price bouncing between pivot levels = range-bound; breaking through = trend day
- **Intraday pivot**: Use today's open, high, low to calculate intraday pivot points
- Entry near pivot S1/S2 = long; entry near pivot R1/R2 = short

## P9: SUPPORT/RESISTANCE LINES
- **Horizontal S/R**: Key price levels where price has reversed before
- **Ascending S/R**: Angled trendlines, rising support or falling resistance
- **Dynamic S/R**: VWAP, 9 EMA, 20 EMA as living S/R levels
- 9 EMA: most widely watched moving average by momentum traders → acts as dynamic resistance/support
- "Very extended off the 9 MA" = increased risk of reversal → use tighter stops

## P10: ALGO SPIKE RULES
- Algorithms drive sudden, sharp moves → spike up, then consolidate/pull back = potential flag
- Algo spikes often happen on news catalyst → combine with P1 catalyst check
- Post-algo pullback entry is the highest-probability play
- Watch for "algo exhaustion" — spike that can't follow through = reversal

## P11: PULLING AWAY PATTERN
- Price consolidates tightly → then breaks out with strong volume and doesn't look back
- Entry: After the break, on any pullback to the consolidation zone
- Confirmed by: Strong candle body, increasing volume, no wicks through the zone

## P12: MA/VWAP PULLBACK RULES
- Price pulls back to 9 EMA or VWAP during a trend → first candle pushing back through = entry
- Same rule: wait for candle close confirmation
- In a strong trend, price respects the 9 EMA as dynamic support (bulls) or resistance (bears)

## P13: CUP AND HANDLE (INTRADAY)
- Intraday version: Rounded bottom → pullback (handle) → break of rim = entry
- Handle pullback must be smaller than the cup depth
- Works best on 5-min chart; same volume rules as daily version

## P14: DOUBLE TOP / DOUBLE BOTTOM
- Two failed attempts to break a level = rejection at the same price twice
- Double top = bearish reversal; Double bottom = bullish reversal
- Entry: On the break of the neckline (valley between the two peaks/troughs)
- Stop: Above/below the rejected level

## P15: HEAD AND SHOULDERS / INVERTED
- Three peaks: left shoulder, higher head, right shoulder (lower than head)
- Neckline = support connecting the two troughs
- Entry: Break of neckline
- Stop: Above right shoulder (for H&S) or below (for inverted)
- Target = distance from head to neckline projected below

## P16: TOP AND BOTTOM REVERSAL
- Reversal = change in sentiment at a key level
- Top reversal: Price was trending up, now rejecting at resistance → bearish signal
- Bottom reversal: Price was trending down, now bouncing off support → bullish signal
- Confirmed by volume spike on the reversal candle

## P17: CURLING PATTERN
- Small consolidation after a spike → price "curls" at the top/bottom
- Subtle pattern — requires watching the 1-min chart closely
- Entry: When the curling candle starts to expand in the direction of the trend
- Often triggered by FDA approval catalyst in biotech stocks

## P18: FRONTSIDE VS BACKSIDE MOMENTUM
- **Frontside**: Riding the direction of the main momentum (WITH the trend)
- **Backside**: Fading the move, trading against momentum toward key levels
- Short sellers typically fade strength (backside) → they short at resistance back toward VWAP
- Day traders: prefer frontside momentum trades = higher probability
- "Backside" trades work best when near a major S/R level

## P19: ROUND TRIPS
- Stock rounds a price level (e.g., $5.00) — passes through, comes back, passes through again
- Round numbers act as psychological support/resistance
- High volume at round numbers = institutional interest
- Trades: Fade the spike through the round number (backside) or confirm breakout past it (frontside)

## P20: SYSTEM SHORT SELLERS
- Systematic short sellers = algos and funds that short on specific conditions
- They cover shorts when price crosses VWAP or fills the gap
- **Short squeeze**: When system short sellers are forced to cover simultaneously → rapid upward spike
- This is why gaps up on high short interest stocks can run so fast — forced covering
- Trade with awareness of short interest and short squeeze potential

## SCANNER PATTERNS (from Bull Flag chapter)
Ross's scanners catch these in order of priority:
1. **Top Gainer** — stocks making the biggest % move today
2. **Small Cap High Day** — low float, big single-day moves
3. **Continuation** — stocks already moving that continue after the open
→ All three feed into the same bull flag / pullback entry setup

## KEY RISK RULES FROM CH5
- **Trap = exit immediately**: Small winner OR small loser — both correct. Holding a losing trap = $50 loss becomes $200 loss
- **Extended off 9 MA = tighter stop**: If stock is far from the 9 EMA, the reversal risk is high
- **Volume inversion = wrong direction**: Light volume on spike + heavy volume on pullback = distribution
- **Don't act on incomplete candles**: Wait for candle close to confirm the signal
- **VWAP is a tug-of-war**: High relative volume + price near VWAP = highest-probability entry zone
