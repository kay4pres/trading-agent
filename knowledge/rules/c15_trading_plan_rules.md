## SECTION 1: THE 7-PHASE APPROACH (Alpha → Real Money)

### Alpha Phase (Current — Simulator)
- **Goal:** gain experience, convert knowledge to skill
- **Share size:** 100 shares (max $10-15 loss feels negligible)
- **Profit target:** +$0.20/share minimum
- **Max loss:** $0.10-0.15/share
- **Trades:** unlimited per day
- **Stock filter:** must meet Five Pillars + the First Pullback setup
- **Time:** sit down at 7am, trade as long as possible

### Beta Phase (Proof of Concept)
- **Goal:** prove consistency before real money
- **Share size:** 100 shares (same as Alpha)
- **Trades:** 1 trade/day maximum — "trade the best, leave the rest"
- **Track separately** from Alpha trades
- **Stop after first trade** — avoid spiraling
- **10-day winning cycle** → ready to fund account and go live
- **Trade reporting per trade:** date, time, P&L (¢/share), did it meet 5 pillars, price at entry, relative volume, float, news (Y/N), timeframe, pullback pattern, hold time
- **Weekly reporting:** total P&L, avg winner (¢/share), avg loser (¢/share), P/L ratio, accuracy
- Cold market = extended Beta Phase, still track separately

### Phase Scaling (after Beta)
- 10 profitable days → scale from 100 → 150 shares
- 10 more profitable days → scale to 200 shares
- Continue 10-day increment scaling
- Ross example: started at $5/day goal, scaled to $100K+ positions over time

### The $200/Day Math

- $1,000/week × 50 weeks = $50,000/year (conservative)
- ×300 rule = equivalent to $15M retirement account

## SECTION 2: THE 5-STEP SYSTEM (Execution Workflow)

### Step 1: Find a Stock with Momentum (Scanner)
- Run scanners: Small Cap High Day, Top Gainer, Continuation
- Confirm it meets Five Pillars
- Confirm it fits the current theme/profile (sub-1M float, reverse split, high SI, AI catalyst, etc.)
- Is it the #1 or top 3-5 most obvious stock today?
- **Rapid "ding ding ding"** = stock repeatedly hitting scanner = strong signal

### Step 2: Check the Catalyst (News)
- Why is the stock moving? Confirm there IS a headline
- Read the headline — understand the catalyst
- Check sector, country code, company fundamentals context
- In hot market: no news but obvious stock = still worth considering
- News can come at: 8am, 9am, top/bottom of hours (8:30, 9:30, etc.)
- **Best windows:** 8-10am pre-market news cycle

### Step 3: Check the Intraday Chart (10s / 1m / 5m)
- **10-second chart:** first few candles after news breaks
- **1-minute chart:** overall momentum
- **5-minute chart:** extended pullback identification
- Look for: long body green candles, higher volume on buy candles vs sell
- Check for: topping tails, jackknife/rejection candles
- Check for: ascending/descending resistance lines
- Fresh news = no prior resistance yet = cleaner setup
- **Volume profile:** high volume buying + low volume selling = bullish
- MACD confirmation: positive = good (especially first 5-10 min after news)
- 9 EMA: stock often pulls back TO the 9 EMA = logical support level

### Step 4: Check the Daily Chart
- Only if intraday chart looks promising (don't skip to daily first)
- Main check: **200-day moving average** resistance
- In high RV stocks: 200 MA can be blown through — awareness, not a hard stop
- Prior resistance levels: flag them but don't let them override RV strength

### Step 5: Check Level 2 + Execute
- **Easy to Borrow vs Hard to Borrow** — HTB = potential short squeeze, prefer HTB
- **SSR (Short Sale Restriction)** — T = threshold (can't short), CB = circuit breaker
- **Marginable vs Non-marginable** — based on broker clearing firm
- **Bid-ask spread** — wider spread = harder to trade, avoid if spread >$0.10
- **Stacked buyers on bid** — 4 buyers vs 1 seller = bullish
- **Time & Sales (Tape):** bursts of green = buying pressure = conviction
- **Green on tape = get in; red = get out or don't enter**

## SECTION 3: THE FIRST PULLBACK SETUP (Core Entry Strategy)

### The Setup
- Buy the **first pullback** after a stock spikes on breaking news
- Can also apply when stock hits High Day scanner (not just breaking news)
- Psychology: rapid move up attracts traders (Pavlovian), first pullback = test of conviction
- Pullback = profit-taking from pre-news holders + early short sellers testing
- If pullback holds 50%+ of the initial move = strong — buyers jump in, shorts cover at highs

### Entry Confirmation
- **First candle to make a NEW HIGH after pullback = THE entry**
- Enter on the candle that BREAKS the high of the pullback candles
- Can **enter early** anticipating breakout:
  - Psychological levels (half-dollar, whole-dollar below apex)
  - Surge of buying on Level 2 (green on tape)
- Early entry = see price moving up almost immediately OR it's a trap
- **If pullback is >4 candles = too weak, skip the trade**
- **If retracement is >50% = too weak, skip**
- **If stop would be >$0.20 = can't maintain 2:1, skip**

### Pullback Tiers (timeframe)
| Tier | Timeframe | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | 10-second | Micro pullback — first 1-2 candles after spike |
| 2 | 1-minute | First 1-minute pullback |
| 3 | 5-minute | Extended pullback — only if still holding 9 EMA |

- **Ross trades:** 1st pullback on 10s, 2nd pullback on 10s, 1st pullback on 1m, 2nd pullback on 1m
- That's 4 potential trades per setup
- Beyond 2nd pullback = diminishing returns
- 5-minute pullbacks are weaker — only trade if stock still holding above 9 EMA

### Stop Loss
- **Primary stop:** low of the pullback pattern (if stop ≤ $0.20)
- **Arbitrary stop:** sometimes use $0.05-0.10 tighter stop to improve P/L ratio
- If stop > $0.20/share = **do not take the trade** (need $0.40+ target to maintain 2:1)
- **Cap losers, let winners run** — opposite of what emotions tell you
- No trailing stop in early phase — just hard stop at defined level

### Profit Target
- **Minimum:** +$0.20/share
- **Preferred:** $0.25-0.50/share
- **Target:** retest of high of day, then extension to next half-dollar/whole-dollar
- **Rule:** must have realistic potential to make 25-50¢/share minimum
- In strong momentum: let it run to next resistance
- Exit indicator overrides target if resistance appears

### Exit Indicators (in priority order)
1. Stock breaks low of pullback = immediate exit
2. Resistance appears (double top forming)
3. Volume profile shifts (high volume selling vs buying)
4. Time-based: if no resolution after ~2 minutes and no new high = exit
5. Pre-defined target hit

## SECTION 4: STOCK SELECTION SWEET SPOT

Within the Five Pillars, Ross's "honey crisp" sweet spot:

| Criterion | Minimum | Ideal / Sweet Spot |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| Price | $2-$20 | **$5-$10** |
| Float | < 20M | **< 5M (micro/nano)** |
| Relative Volume | 5× | **10× or higher** |
| % Up Today | 10%+ | **50%+** |
| Catalyst | Some news | **Fresh breaking news** |

- Top 3-5 gainers in the market = ideal candidates
- If stock barely meets criteria (19.99M float, $19.99 price, 5× RV, 10% up) = LOW probability
- If stock is exceptional (1M float, $7, 60% up, 10× RV, breaking news) = HIGH probability
- Current theme matters: if last mover had sub-1M float, look for sub-1M float next

## SECTION 5: PRE-TRADING CHECKLIST

Before every trading session:
1. **Mental check:** Am I in the right headspace? Did I sleep well? Am I calm?
2. **Market condition:** Is the market hot, cold, or neutral?
3. **My strategy:** Have the last few days confirmed my strategy is working?
4. **Environment:** Quiet workspace, no distractions, phone off
5. **Charts printed** around workspace — visual reinforcement of the First Pullback pattern
6. **Trading plan printed** and sticky notes over P&L so you focus on process not profit
7. **P&L hidden** during trade execution to avoid emotional decisions
