## ROSS CAMERON — ANATOMY OF A STRONG DAILY CHART

### The 6 Pillars (daily chart requirements)
Ross says he tests each stock against 6 criteria on the daily chart — look for:
1. Clean price action (no noise, clear trends)
2. Volume confirmation (high relative volume today)
3. Catalyst presence (news, earnings, FDA, etc.)
4. Support/resistance clarity
5. Favorable risk/reward setup
6. Tradeable price range ($2-$20 typically)

### Daily Chart Entry Triggers
- **First candle making new high** — key phrase from transcript
- **Full flags** — bull flag pattern on daily
- **Flat top breakouts** — consolidation at resistance → breakout
- **Flat bottom breakdowns** — bear version
- **ABCD patterns** — measured move patterns
- **Head and shoulders** — reversal pattern
- **Cup and handle** — continuation pattern
- **First pullback** — wait for the pullback after a move

### Volume Rules
- "Today's volume should be significantly higher than average"
- "High relative volume" = volume today vs. what's normal for that stock
- Low volume consolidation = building energy for breakout
- Volume spike confirms the catalyst

### Key Quote
"A daily chart looks good does not mean that I'll buy that stock."
— Ross, on the importance of intraday confirmation

### Catalyst on Daily Chart
- Inter-day catalyst (breaking news) creates the move
- "Unless you have a fresh breaking news catalyst" — can't trade the daily alone
- The daily chart explains WHY the stock moved
- The intraday confirms the entry

### Resistance Levels
- "The daily chart is the reason that the stock will stop moving higher"
- Support becomes resistance after break
- Look for prior highs acting as resistance
