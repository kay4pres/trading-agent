## P1: CHART TYPES & TIMEFRAMES
- **Candlestick chart**: Shows open, high, low, close — preferred for day trading
- **Timeframes**: 1-min (precise entry/exit), 5-min (primary trade setup), 15-min (context), daily (longer-term direction)
- **Multiple timeframes**: Always check a higher timeframe for context before trading on a lower timeframe
- Ross's primary intraday: **5-min for setup, 1-min for entry timing**

## P2: CANDLESTICK BASICS
- **Green/white candle**: Close > Open = bullish bias for that candle
- **Red/black candle**: Close < Open = bearish bias for that candle
- **Candle body**: The real price movement (open to close)
- **Upper wick**: High reached but rejected
- **Lower wick**: Low found but rejected
- **Doji**: Open ≈ Close = indecision — watch for reversal
- **Hammer**: Small body + long lower wick = bullish reversal (at support)
- **Shooting star**: Small body + long upper wick = bearish reversal (at resistance)
- Candle color context: In an uptrend, red candles = pullback; in a downtrend, green candles = bounce

## P3: MULTI-CANDLESTICK PATTERNS
- **Engulfing**: Current candle body fully engulfs prior candle body
  - Bullish engulfing: green candle engulfs red → bullish reversal
  - Bearish engulfing: red candle engulfs green → bearish reversal
- **Morning star**: Red → small body → green (3-candle bullish reversal at bottom)
- **Evening star**: Green → small body → red (3-candle bearish reversal at top)
- **Three white soldiers**: 3 consecutive green candles with higher closes = strong bullish continuation
- **Three black crows**: 3 consecutive red candles with lower closes = strong bearish continuation
- **Inside candle**: Current candle is fully within prior candle's range = consolidation → breakout coming
- **Outside candle**: Current candle fully engulfs prior candle = momentum shift
- **Volume confirmation**: Pattern only valid with volume on the confirmation candle

## P4: SUPPORT & RESISTANCE
- **Support**: Price level where buying interest has historically exceeded selling → price bounces up
- **Resistance**: Price level where selling interest has historically exceeded buying → price bounces down
- **Role reversal**: When support is broken, it becomes resistance (and vice versa)
- **Key support/resistance**: Prior highs/lows, VWAP, 9 EMA, round numbers, gap edges
- **Consolidation zones**: 3+ touches of same level = stronger S/R when broken
- **Width of consolidation**: Wider = more significant the eventual breakout
- **First pullback to broken S/R**: After a breakout, price often pulls back to retest the broken level

## P5: GAPS & WINDOWS
- **Gap**: Price jumps over a range with no trading in between
- **Gap up**: Price opens above prior close → bullish gap = strength
- **Gap down**: Price opens below prior close → bearish gap = weakness
- **Gap fill**: Price moves back into the gap area = filling the gap
- **Common saying**: "95% of gaps fill" — but NOT all gaps fill immediately
- **Types of gaps**:
  - Common gap: Smaller, uneventful, often fills quickly
  - Breakaway gap: Occurs at breakout from consolidation — strong signal, less likely to fill
  - Runaway/measuring gap: Occurs mid-trend — confirms trend strength, used to measure targets
  - Exhaustion gap: Occurs at end of trend — signals reversal coming
- **Gap fill entry**: Wait for pullback into the gap zone, enter on first candle making new high/low
- **Gap and go**: Don't chase — let it pull back first; chasing gaps = getting stopped out by afternoon sellers

## P6: MULTI-TIMEFRAME ALIGNMENT
- **Trend direction**: Use daily chart to determine if stock is in an uptrend, downtrend, or range
- **Setup timeframe**: 5-min chart to identify the specific entry pattern
- **Entry timeframe**: 1-min chart to time the actual entry
- **Rule**: All three timeframes should agree
  - Daily: Uptrend + 5-min: Bull flag forming + 1-min: Pullback to 9 EMA = high-probability long
  - Daily: Uptrend + 5-min: Bull flag forming + 1-min: Extended from 9 EMA = skip
- **Higher timeframe S/R**: Daily HOD/LOD = significant intraday levels
- **Opening range**: First 15-30 minutes set the intraday range; use this to gauge the day's character

## P7: TECHNICAL INDICATORS
- **VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)**: Average price weighted by volume — THE most important intraday indicator
  - Above VWAP = bullish; below VWAP = bearish
  - Price crossing VWAP = shift in control
- **9 EMA (Exponential Moving Average)**: Short-term trend line — dynamic support/resistance
  - Price above 9 EMA = short-term bullish; below = short-term bearish
  - "Very extended off the 9 MA = increased risk of false breakout"
- **20 EMA**: Slightly longer-term trend reference
- **RSI (Relative Strength Index)**: Momentum oscillator, 0-100
  - Overbought (>70) = reversal risk; Oversold (<30) = bounce risk
  - Divergence: Price makes new high but RSI doesn't = bearish divergence
- **Volume**: Confirms price action — high volume + price move = real; low volume = weak
- **MACD**: Trend momentum — histogram shows acceleration/deceleration of trend
- **ADX**: Measures trend strength, not direction
  - ADX > 25 = trending; ADX < 20 = range-bound
- **ATR (Average True Range)**: Measures volatility — use for position sizing and stop placement
  - High ATR = wider stop needed; Low ATR = tighter stop possible

## P8: STRONG VS WEAK DAILY CHARTS
- **Strong chart characteristics**:
  - Stock in uptrend (higher highs, higher lows)
  - Gap up on catalyst at open
  - Holding above VWAP throughout the day
  - Making higher highs and higher lows
  - Volume increasing on up days, decreasing on pullbacks
  - Consolidating at higher levels rather than lower levels
- **Weak chart characteristics**:
  - Stock in downtrend (lower highs, lower lows)
  - Gap down on bad news
  - Below VWAP throughout the day
  - Making lower highs and lower lows
  - Volume increasing on down days
  - Consolidating at lower levels
- **Don't fight the daily chart**: If daily is weak, only trade the short side; if strong, only trade long
- **Gap down stock**: Can still be a good long IF it reclaims VWAP and shows strength — don't fade everything

## P9: CHART SETUP
- **Required on TradingView**: 5-min chart, 1-min chart, daily chart, VWAP, 9 EMA, 20 EMA
- **News feed**: Bloomberg, Benzinga, Twitter/X catalyst scanning
- **Scanner**: Warrior scanner (or Fincept + custom filters)
- **Key filters**:
  - Price: $2–$20 (optimal for day trading)
  - Float: Nano/Micro/Low float preferred
  - Relative volume: >2x average
  - Catalyst: News confirming the move
  - SSR (Short Sale Restriction): If triggered, limits shorting — avoid short entries when SSR on
- **Trade journal**: Track every entry, exit, P&L, and rule that was applied
  - "Most traders who fail have not put in the reps. Paper trade until you are consistently profitable."
- **Platform hotkeys**: Learn order entry hotkeys — speed is critical in day trading

## MASTER RULE: READING THE FULL PICTURE
1. Start with **daily chart**: Uptrend or downtrend? Gap direction? Catalyst?
2. Move to **5-min chart**: Is a bull flag / consolidation forming? Volume profile correct?
3. Check **VWAP + 9 EMA**: Where is price relative to these key levels?
4. Confirm with **RSI/MACD**: Momentum confirming the direction?
5. Enter on **1-min**: First candle making a new high/low after pullback
6. Exit on **1-min reversal**: Don't hold through a 1-min candle close against you

→ If all 6 steps agree = take the trade
→ If any step conflicts = skip or wait
