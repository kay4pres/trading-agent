## ROSS CAMERON — STOCK TYPES

### How Ross Finds Stocks (Scanner-Driven)
"9 out of 10 times, the stock is hitting one of my scanners"
Ross uses:
- **Small Cap High Day scanner** — main trigger
- **Top Gainer scanner** — for gap-ups
- **Continuation scanner** — specifically for continuation setups

Scanner columns: name, price, volume, float, relative volume

### Float Types (by preference for day trading)
1. **Nano float** — < 1M shares — explosive but risky
2. **Micro float** — 1-5M shares — ideal for Ross
3. **Low float** — 5-20M shares — good
4. **Medium float** — 20-100M shares — harder to move
5. **Large cap** — 100M+ shares — institutional, slower

### Hard to Borrow / Easy to Borrow
- Hard to borrow = more likely to short squeeze
- Easy to borrow = can short freely
- SSR (Short Sale Restriction) = cannot short on up-tick — check daily
- Borrow rate = fee for borrowing shares to short

### Gap Types
- **Gap and go** — full gap up, continues higher (ideal)
- **Gap fill** — gap up then fades back to fill the gap (fade the gap)
- **Partial gap** — gap partially filled, then continues
- **Full gap** — completely gapped past previous close
- "Fading the gap" = shorting the overextension after a gap

### Volume
- 1 billion shares of volume (1B with a B) = institutional names
- Small cap: 1M-10M shares normal
- Nano cap: light volume normally, volume spike = big move

### Large Cap vs Small Cap
- Large cap: "loyal following of traders and investors"
- Large caps trade closely together (correlated)
- Small caps = individual moves, less correlation
