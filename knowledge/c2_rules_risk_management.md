## NEW: 5 Types of Risk (Ch2)

### 1. Hard Stop Risk
- Distance between entry and max loss = primary risk
- **Rule:** If not profitable within **2 minutes** → bailout ("breakout or bailout")
- **Rule:** Keep stops as **tight as possible**
- **Rule:** Profit target = **2× what you're risking** (minimum 2:1 ratio)
  - Risk 5¢ → target 10¢
  - Risk 10¢ → target 20¢

### 2. Exposure Risk
- Formula: share size × $ per share
- **The longer you hold, the more exposure risk you take**
- Use smaller size for longer holds
- Commission-free brokers: can split pennies, lower risk per trade
- Lower accuracy with commission-free brokers is OK — losses stay tiny

### 3. Float & Volatility Risk
- Lower float = higher volatility = more risk
- Higher float = lower volatility
- **Rule:** Want volatility (rewards) BUT need liquidity (safety)
- Low float + low volume = big spreads = dangerous
- Stocks can move 50% up OR 50% down in a single day

### 4. Stock Halt Risk
- Circuit breaker halts: 5–15 minutes (common, daily)
- **News pending halts: can last hours** (rare — 99% of news doesn't cause halt)
- **High risk:** Stock up 100%+ on NO news → risk of news pending halt
- If stock halts, company announces "no news" → stock opens and crashes

### 5. Mindset Risk
- Improper position sizing → emotional trading
- **Rule:** Start with **100 shares** until consistency is proven
- FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) → chasing
- Revenge trading → trading from emotion, not logic
- **Averaging down = NEVER a good strategy**
- Social media comparison = thief of joy

## NEW: Daily Scaling Strategy

1. Start each day at **¼ size** of full position
2. Build cushion equal to **¼ of daily goal** before increasing size
3. Once in profit: risk going to break-even, not going red
4. After first **10%+ loss of daily goal in one trade** → reduce size back down
5. **Trade MORE on green days, trade LESS on red days** (counterintuitive)

### Ross's Metrics (from his real trading):
- Average winner: ~18¢/share (after scaling change)
- Average loser: ~15¢/share
- Green day accuracy: ~70%
- Red day accuracy: ~46%
- → Red days come from: overtrading, revenge trading, cold market

## 10 Rules to Take Away from Ch2

1. Set your stop BEFORE taking any trade
2. No trade without 2:1 profit/loss potential
3. Start with 100 shares, prove consistency, then scale up
4. Never average down — cut the loss and move on
5. Breakout or bailout: profitable in 2 min or exit
6. Avoid stocks up big with no news (halt risk)
7. Use ¼ size to start the day, scale up after building a cushion
8. Reduce size immediately after a 10%+ drawdown in one trade
9. Journal your emotions — name the feeling before it hijacks you
10. Scale from $20/day → $40/day → 100 shares → 200 shares → 400 shares → ...
